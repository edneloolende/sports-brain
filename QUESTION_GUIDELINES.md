# Sports Brain — Question Writing Guidelines

## Core Principle

**Lead with the fact, not the identity.** A clue should describe what the person *did*, not who they *are*. Strip away the preamble. The fact itself should do the work.

---

## Rule 1: Nationality — remove by default, keep only when it genuinely helps

Nationality is almost always the first thing to cut. It narrows the answer too easily and rewards the wrong kind of knowledge. Remove it in 95% of cases.

The rare exception: when the player is obscure enough that the clue would otherwise be impossibly vague, and nationality is the only available anchor. Or when the nationality itself is part of what makes the moment notable — a surprising or culturally significant detail. When in doubt, cut it.

| ❌ Before | ✅ After |
|-----------|----------|
| "French striker who scored..." | "Scored..." |
| "Brazilian goalkeeper who joined..." | "Joined..." |
| "Argentine manager who ended..." | "Ended..." |

---

## Rule 2: Remove position words unless they genuinely disambiguate

Position words (striker, midfielder, goalkeeper) are usually redundant. Remove them. But keep or restore them when the position is the *only* thing separating the answer from several other plausible options.

| Example | Decision | Reason |
|---------|----------|--------|
| "French striker who became Arsenal's all-time record scorer" → **"Arsenal's all-time record scorer"** | Remove | No one else is Arsenal's all-time record scorer |
| "English centre-back who won six Premier League titles at Manchester United" → **"Defender who won six Premier League titles at Manchester United"** | Keep | Multiple Man Utd players won six PL titles — position narrows it |
| "Player who won a record 13 PL titles" (GIGGS) | Keep "Player" | Without it, the clue reads like a riddle with no anchor |

**Test:** Could a different player fit the clue without the position word? If yes, keep it.

---

## Rule 3: Club names — keep when they're the anchor, cut when redundant

Club names stay when they're the essential identifying context. They go when the achievement alone is specific enough.

| Example | Decision | Reason |
|---------|----------|--------|
| "Scored 14 PL goals for Arsenal in 2022/23" (SAKA) | Keep Arsenal | The goal tally alone doesn't identify him |
| "Joined Chelsea from Real Madrid in 2021, winning the PL title" (KOVACIC) | Keep both clubs | Without a club anchor, too many players have moved between big clubs |
| "Scored PL goals for four different clubs, including Coventry City and Aston Villa" (DUBLIN) | Add clubs back | Vague achievement needs anchors |

**Test:** If you removed the club name, could the clue apply to more than two or three players? If yes, the club stays.

---

## Rule 4: Use verbs to distinguish managers from players

When a clue could describe either a player or a manager, the verb does the work. Words like *won* and *scored* imply a player. Words like *led*, *guided*, and *managed* imply a manager. Always frame manager clues with language that signals leadership and agency — never passive participation.

| ❌ Ambiguous | ✅ Clear |
|-------------|---------|
| "Won the Premier League with Chelsea in 2004/05" | "Led Chelsea to the Premier League title in 2004/05" |
| "Won the FA Cup with Wigan in 2013" | "Guided Wigan to FA Cup glory in 2013" |
| "Won Leicester's miraculous 5000-1 title in 2015/16" | "Guided Leicester to their miraculous 5000-1 title win" |

This applies any time the answer is a manager. The framing should make the role unambiguous without having to state it explicitly.

---

## Rule 5: Include years/seasons when the event is not unique

Years disambiguate. Include them when:
- The same achievement happened in multiple seasons (Arsenal had two Double seasons — always specify 2001/02 for Pires)
- The stat alone doesn't narrow the timeframe enough ("Won the Golden Boot with two different clubs *in the late 90s and early 2000s*")
- The player had multiple notable stints at different clubs

Omit years when:
- The achievement is one-of-a-kind ("Arsenal's all-time record scorer")
- The clue is deliberately cryptic ("Upturned collar")

---

## Rule 6: The clue must be uniquely answerable

After stripping, always check: *Could a different player be the answer?* If the stripped clue is too open-ended, add back the minimum context needed — one club, one year, one specific detail — to make the answer unambiguous.

---

## Difficulty Scale

The scale runs 1–5. The key calibration: **err harder**. Most well-known players should be 3 or 4, not 1 or 2. Reserve 1 and 2 for genuinely iconic, instantly-recalled facts.

---

### ★☆☆☆☆ — 1: Instantly recognisable

Any engaged football fan gets this immediately. The biggest names, the most iconic moments, the most obvious nicknames for top clubs.

**Examples:**
- "Arsenal's all-time record scorer" → HENRY
- "13 Premier League titles with Manchester United" → FERGUSON
- "93rd-minute goal vs QPR on the final day to win the title" → AGUERO
- "Last line of defence in Man Utd's 1998/99 treble-winning side" → SCHMEICHEL
- "Ended Liverpool's 30-year wait for a league title" → KLOPP
- Club nicknames for the biggest clubs: GUNNERS, REDS, BLUES, HAMMERS

---

### ★★☆☆☆ — 2: Known to most PL fans

Famous players and facts that most people who follow the Premier League would know, but require a moment's thought.

**Examples:**
- "Broke the PL record with 32 goals in 2017/18" → SALAH
- "Manchester United's all-time record scorer" → ROONEY
- "Led Chelsea to the Premier League title in 2004/05" → MOURINHO
- "Attracted a then-world record £85m bid from Real Madrid in 2013" → BALE
- "Player who won a record 13 PL titles" → GIGGS
- Club nicknames for well-known but non-elite clubs: TOFFEES, FOXES, HORNETS

---

### ★★★☆☆ — 3: Solid PL knowledge required

Facts that require real engagement with the league — not just knowing the famous players, but knowing what they specifically did.

**Examples:**
- "211 Chelsea goals" → LAMPARD
- "Scored the equaliser in the 2012 Champions League final" → DROGBA
- "Won the 2012 Champions League with Chelsea as caretaker manager" → DIMATTEO
- "Defender who won six Premier League titles at Manchester United" → FERDINAND
- "Guided Blackburn to their only Premier League title" → DALGLISH
- "Football Writers' Player of the Year in Arsenal's 2001/02 Double season" → PIRES

---

### ★★★★☆ — 4: Deep PL knowledge

Obscure stats, lesser-known players, or famous players with non-obvious clues. Someone who watched closely will know — casual fans will struggle.

**Examples:**
- "PFA Player of the Year in 2016/17" → KANTE
- "Won the Golden Boot with two different clubs in the late 90s and early 2000s" → HASSELBAINK
- "Shearer's SAS strike partner at Blackburn" → SUTTON
- "Missed the 1999 Champions League final through suspension" → KEANE
- "Scored a Guinness World Record hat-trick in 2:56" → MANE
- "Holds the all-time record for most PL assists by a foreign player" → FABREGAS

---

### ★★★★★ — 5: Elite or deliberately cryptic

Either maximally stripped clues where the fact is well-known but the clue is oblique, or genuinely niche facts that only dedicated historians of the league would know.

**Examples:**
- "Upturned collar" → CANTONA *(single iconic image, no other context)*
- "31 PL goals for Liverpool in 2013/14" → SUAREZ *(famous record, stripped of all identity)*
- "Penalty saves in the 2005 CL final shootout won the trophy for Liverpool" → DUDEK
- "Scored Liverpool's third in the 2005 Champions League final comeback" → ALONSO *(first or second = easier; third = very obscure)*
- "Headed the winner in the 2013 FA Cup final vs Man City" → WATSON *(barely remembered)*
- "Hat-trick vs Barcelona in the 1997/98 Champions League" → ASPRILLA

---

## Difficulty spread within a day

Every day does not need to hit all five ratings. A day can skew harder or easier overall. But nearly every day should have at least one question at difficulty 1 or 2 — typically the club nickname — to give players a foothold before the harder questions hit. Think of it as a natural escalation, not a formula to fill. A day that runs 1, 3, 3, 4, 5 is perfectly fine. A day with no question below a 3 should be the rare exception.

---

## Quick checklist before publishing a clue

1. Does it open with a nationality? → Remove it (unless it's the only anchor for an obscure player)
2. Does it open with a position word? → Remove unless needed to disambiguate
3. Is the answer a manager? → Use *led*, *guided*, *managed* — not *won*
4. Is the club name doing real work, or is it redundant? → Keep if it's the anchor; cut if the fact stands alone
5. Could a different player plausibly answer this? → If yes, add one specific detail
6. Does the year/season matter? → Include if there's any risk of ambiguity
7. Is the difficulty honest? → Famous player + specific obscure fact = at least a 3. When in doubt, go harder.
